An Essay in Probability  ·  Football, 1872 — 2026

The Miracle Index

When supporters say “it was written,” they reach for fate. But every result was once only probable.

There is a quiet arrogance in hindsight. A team wins against the odds and we call it destiny, as though the outcome had been waiting all along. Yet before the whistle, the world was open — and most of what felt inevitable was, in truth, unlikely. This is an attempt to measure that unlikelihood: to take a century and a half of football, ask what each result was supposed to be, and find the moments when the impossible quietly happened anyway.

The Deeper Miracle

Surviving the tournament

A single upset is one thing; climbing through an entire bracket against the odds is another. We froze every team's strength at the eve of Qatar 2022 — no hindsight — and simulated the whole tournament forty thousand times, then asked how improbable each nation's actual run truly was.

Method

How the unlikely is measured

An Elo rating model, in the tradition of the World Football Elo Ratings, is built chronologically across every international match since 1872. Each team carries a strength; each result moves it, weighted by the importance of the fixture and the margin of victory. Home advantage is set at one hundred points, and falls to zero on neutral ground.

Before any World Cup match, the model holds an expectation for each side — a number between zero and one. When the underdog wins, the miracle index is simply one minus that expectation, expressed out of a hundred. A team expected to take a quarter of a point that wins outright has authored a far greater miracle than a near-equal that edges through.

For the deeper question — how improbable a whole run was — the same ratings are frozen at the eve of the tournament and the entire bracket is simulated forty thousand times. Group stages are played as round-robins; knockout ties resolve in regulation, and when level, a penalty shootout is modelled as a near coin-flip with only a mild edge to the stronger side. The qualification miracle is one minus the probability of reaching the stage a team actually reached.
